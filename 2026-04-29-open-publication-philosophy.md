# The Philosophy — Richard Lofthouse

**Coined:** 2026-04-29  
**Status:** Active — evolving

---

## The Core

> **Publish everything. Keep nothing locked. Speed and timestamps beat patents. The more you give away, the more you own.**

---

## Why It Works

### Patents are for people with lawyers. Timestamps are for everyone.

A patent costs £5-15K, takes 2-3 years, and requires a lawyer to enforce. A GitHub commit costs nothing and is legally meaningful the moment it's made. For an independent creator, the timestamp *is* the IP protection.

### The more you give away, the more you own.

Counterintuitive but true. When you publish a concept publicly:
- You become the person who named it
- You establish prior art — no one can lock it down after you
- You build a track record of being first
- You keep the space open for everyone, which builds goodwill

Hoarding ideas is a losing strategy for someone without capital to enforce them. Publishing is the winning one.

### Speed is the moat.

Funded startups are slow. They have meetings. They have roadmaps. They have lawyers who review everything before it ships.

You have none of that. You can go from idea to published spec to live GitHub repo in under an hour. That speed — consistently applied — is a genuine competitive advantage.

### The compounding effect.

Every published concept adds to a body of work. One idea is a footnote. Ten ideas is a pattern. Fifty ideas is a body of work that gets you invited to speak, consulted, and taken seriously.

The record compounds. The reputation compounds. The prior art compounds.

### You can't enforce what you don't publish.

Keeping an idea secret protects nothing. If someone else has the same idea independently and publishes first, you've lost. If they patent it, you've lost. If they build it and you're unknown, you've lost.

Publishing is the only move that gives you any leverage at all.

---

## The Practice

1. **Have an idea** — anything that feels novel, named, or architectural
2. **Spec it quickly** — a markdown file, not a dissertation
3. **Commit it** — GitHub timestamp is automatic and immutable
4. **Publish it** — article, tweet, or both
5. **Move on** — the record is set, now build the next one

---

## What This Is Not

This is not "give everything away for free forever." The CC BY-NC-SA licence means:
- Personal and research use: free
- Commercial use: requires permission from you

You're not donating to corporations. You're keeping the space open while retaining commercial leverage.

## The Reminder

When you're sitting on an idea, ask yourself:

*"Who benefits from me keeping this private?"*

If the answer isn't obvious — publish it.
